Country music’s most enduring legend, Boone Hargrove, has defied time itself. Born in the swamps of Louisiana in 1948, Hargrove became a household name in the 1970s with his soulful voice, gritty lyrics, and undeniable charisma. However, his meteoric rise was cut short in 1976 when he fell into a mysterious coma after reportedly taking a handful of pills handed to him by none other than Jerry Lee Lewis.
Nearly half a century later, the impossible has happened— Boone Hargrove has awoken. And now, the man once dubbed "The Swamp's Son" is picking up his guitar and stepping back into the spotlight. Hargrove's comeback is marked by the release of his brand-new album, Ramblin’ Man’s Lament. A heartfelt ode to lost time, redemption, and the ever-changing tides of life, the album blends Hargrove’s signature swamp-country sound with a modern edge. Backed by his new band, The Ridge Runners, Boone is hitting the road again, bringing his legendary storytelling and magnetic stage presence to audiences nationwide. “This isn’t just a second chance,” Hargrove said in a recent interview. “This is a resurrection. I’ve got stories to tell and songs to sing, and I can’t wait to share them with the world.”
Boone Hargrove’s Ramblin’ Man’s Lament is available now on all major streaming platforms, with vinyl editions available through select retailers.
